Operation: Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy

Indication: Gallstones, previous cholecystitis


Findings:

  • Thin-walled gallbladder.


Incision and Approach:

  • Infraumbilical Hasson's open entry 10mm port.
  • Epigastric port 10mm, right upper quadrant and right flank 5mm ports all under direct vision


Procedure:

  • Peritoneum over gallbladder opened anteriorly and posteriorly.
  • Calot's triangle identified and critical view of safety achieved using combination of hook diathermy and blunt dissection.
  • Cystic duct clipped (2+1) with 10mm ligaclip and cut in between.
  • Cystic artery clipped (2+1) with 10mm ligaclip and cut in between.
  • Gallbladder removed from liver bed with diathermy and retrieved via BERT bag through umbilical port under direct vision.
  • Local washout to RUQ.
  • Haemostasis achieved.
  • Ports removed under direct vision.


Closure:

  • Z-shaped J-PDS to umbilical fascia (finger sweep clear).
  • Subcuticular 3-0 monocryl.
  • Simple dressing.


Specimens:

  • 1x gallbladder histopathology.


Post-operative Instructions:

  • Routine post-operative observations
  • Analgesia
  • Mobilise
  • Eat and drink as tolerated
  • No heavy lifting for 6-8 weeks
  • Aim home today when pain controlled and passes urine

What structure crosses Calot's triangle?

  • Portal vein
  • Cystic artery
  • Common bile duct
