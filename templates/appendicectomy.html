Operation: Laparoscopic Appendicectomy

Indication: Acute appendicitis


Findings:

  • Acutely inflamed appendix.
  • No perforation / collection / necrosis.
  • Mild turbid free fluid in the pelvis.


Incision and Approach:

  • Infraumbilical Hasson's open entry 10mm port.
  • 5mm left iliac fossa and suprapubilc ports under direct vision.


Procedure:

  • Appendix skeletalised from mesoappendix with hook diathermy from tip until base.
  • Endoloops (2+1) to base and appendix cut in between.
  • Appendix retrieved through umbilical port via BERT bag under direct vision.
  • Local washout to pelvis and right iliac fossa.
  • Haemostasis achieved.
  • Ports removed under direct vision


Closure:

  • Z-shaped J-PDS to umbilical fascia (finger sweep clear).
  • Subcuticular 3-0 monocryl.
  • Simple dressing.


Specimens:

  • 1x appendix histopathology.


Post-operative Instructions:

  • Routine post-operative observations
  • Analgesia
  • Mobilise
  • Eat and drink as tolerated
  • 2 further doses of antibiotics
  • No heavy lifting for 6-8 weeks
  • Aim home tomorrow if well

Where does the appendicular artery originate from?

  • Ileocolic artery
  • Superior mesenteric artery
  • Inferior mesenteric artery
